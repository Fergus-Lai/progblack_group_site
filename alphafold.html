AlphaFold

The Protein Folding Problem

Proteins are the building blocks of life, each with a unique 3D structure. For the previous 50 years experimentally figuring out the specific structure of proteins has often taken many years and significant amounts of money. There are over 200 million proteins known to science, the structure and function of which may provide insights into life and aid in development of medicines. However, until recently, it was only possible to study the structures of a tiny fraction of these proteins.

Open Source

In July 2021, Nature published a paper on the methodology of AlphaFold, and DeepMind open sourced the code and an accessible database of the predictions, which was later expanded in 2022 to the point where it now indexes predictions of over 200 million proteins.

How AlphaFold Solves It

Using technology perfected when AlphaGo beat Lee Sedol, Google's DeepMind began development of a machine learning model which would predict the 3D structure of proteins. Two years later in 2018, the model was first tested publically achieving nearly 60% accuracy in the prediction of a protein structure, higher than any human team or machine learning model. The next year, this was beaten by AlphaFold 2 at over 80%.
